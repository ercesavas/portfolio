What you built — 3 tiers of MVP models
Tier 1 — Simple rank sum (6 variants)
Rank each player 1–6 per stat, sum the ranks, lowest total = MVP. You tested every combination of raw rbs_pg vs rebounding %, individual shooting % vs eFG, with and without eFG. Clean and interpretable. Result: Marwan 1st or tied in most variants.
Tier 2 — Unweighted z-score composite (5 variants)
Standardise each stat to z-scores, sum positives, subtract negatives. Captures magnitude of differences, not just ordering. Erce jumps to 1st consistently — his extreme win_pct (67%) produces a large z-score.
Tier 3 — Weighted z-score (4 variants)
Same as Tier 2 but multiplies each z-score by a custom weight. Shooting efficiency (1.8–2.0) and win_pct (1.7) weighted highest, blk_pg lowest (0.7–0.8). Erce pulls further ahead.
Tier 4 — Consensus score
Blends the best weighted z-score (60%) with PIE z-score (40%). Bridges the box-score model and the game-impact model. Final ranking: Erce → Marwan → Albroz → Jackson → Jalal → Moe.

What I'd do differently — category-grouped weighting
Your approach weights individual stats. Mine groups stats into 7 basketball pillars first, then allocates a budget to each pillar. This prevents a category from being over-represented just because it has more columns — e.g. having pct_1pt + pct_2pt + efg_mean in the same model triple-counts shooting efficiency.

What you got right

Z-scores over raw ranks captures magnitude not just ordering. Testing multiple feature combinations is good robustness practice. Blending box-score with PIE in the consensus is the most sophisticated thing you did and it is genuinely good methodology.

One thing to watch

Win% is a team stat — Erce's 67% is partly driven by his pairing with Jackson (87.5% duo rate). Players with fewer games like Jackson (21) can also have more extreme per-game numbers. Consider a minimum game threshold before inclusion.

The honest truth about any of these models
With only 6 players, z-scores are computed on a very small population — each player's score is heavily influenced by who else is in the group. The results are meaningful rankings within this group but shouldn't be over-interpreted as absolute grades. The top 3 (Erce → Marwan → Albroz) are consistent across every model, which is the real signal. The ordering at 4th/5th is noisier and more model-dependent.
